A Strange Arrival

The night was silent, except for the scratching of a pen against paper. Neorock, a 13-year-old boy, sat at his desk, flipping through the pages of his textbook. The dim glow of the study lamp cast shadows around his small room.

Suddenly, a deep humming sound filled the air, making the walls tremble. Neorock’s pen slipped from his fingers. The air around him grew heavy as a shimmering blue portal ripped open in the middle of the room.

Neorock stumbled backward, his heart pounding. From the swirling energy, a tall figure emerged, draped in a flowing black cape. His silver eyes gleamed under the hood, his presence radiating power.

Neorock froze. His body screamed at him to run, but he forced himself to speak.

“W-Who are you?!” he stammered.

The figure took a step forward, his voice calm but commanding.

"I am Phantom. I have come from another universe to find you."

Neorock’s mind spun. "Another… universe?"

Phantom nodded. “Yes. Your world, Earth, is part of the Milky Way in this multiverse. But I come from a different universe, one from the Nexus Universe.”

Neorock narrowed his eyes, still unsure whether to trust this stranger. "What do you want from me?"

Phantom took a deep breath. "I have come to take you to Herlek, a planet in my universe. There is no time to explain everything here. The demon lord Arengoth is taking over the multiverse, and the Milky Way is next. You must come with me."

Neorock’s chest tightened. "Wait… taking over? You mean Earth is in danger?"

Phantom’s gaze darkened. “Not just in danger. Earth and the entire Milky Way are already under attack. You may not have seen it yet, but I can hear them. The demons have arrived.”

Just as Phantom finished speaking, the ground shook violently. A low, monstrous growl echoed from outside.

Neorock’s breathing grew uneven. This couldn’t be real. This had to be a dream, right?

Phantom extended his hand. "Neorock, you must choose now. Stay here and be consumed by darkness, or come with me and fight for the multiverse."

Neorock clenched his fists. His heart told him the choice was already made.

"I’ll go with you," he said firmly.

Phantom gave a single nod. “Then let’s go.”

The Multiverse Gateway

The swirling energy of the portal surrounded them, and in a flash, they landed on a massive floating platform.

Neorock gasped as he took in the sight—endless portals, each one leading to a different universe. Above each portal, a glowing nameplate displayed the universe’s name. Below the names, floating screens showed the date, time, year, and planet selector.

"Where are we?" Neorock asked.

“This is the Multiverse Gateway,” Phantom explained. “It is the center of the multiverse. Every universe is connected through this place.”

Neorock noticed Phantom walking toward a particular portal. It was marked:

NEXUS UNIVERSE

Planet: Herlek

Phantom adjusted the settings to Herlek, and without hesitation, they stepped inside.

Arrival at Herlek

The air around them shimmered, and within seconds, they emerged onto a vast futuristic world.

Neorock’s mouth fell open. The city before him was breathtaking—tall, sleek buildings lined with glowing blue circuits, hovering vehicles moving smoothly in the sky, and lush green forests mixed with modern technology. Rivers glowed with an ethereal light, and mountains stood tall, their peaks touching the sky.

"This is… incredible," Neorock whispered.

Phantom’s face remained serious. "Yes, but Herlek was not always like this."

Neorock turned to him. "What do you mean?"

Phantom let out a deep breath.

The fall of the Multiverse

"Many years ago, the multiverse was peaceful. The Nexus Universe was its center, and Herlek was the most advanced planet of all. Our people had technology beyond your imagination, but we also lived in harmony with nature.

But then… the darkness came."

Neorock stiffened. "Darkness?"

Phantom nodded. "The demon lord Arengoth. His army rose from the depths of the multiverse, conquering one universe after another. We fought back, but it was not enough.

Our Nexus Universe controlled the multiverse, except for one universe—Parlent. That was already a land of demons. It became their base, their stronghold. And from there, they attacked."

Neorock swallowed hard. "And you lost?"

Phantom clenched his fists. "We lost everything. Herlek fell. Its people were wiped out."

Neorock stared at the ground. "But if Herlek was destroyed, then how is this city here?"

Phantom’s silver eyes softened. “Because me and my friend Vault survived. We rebuilt what we could. But Herlek is not truly safe. The only thing keeping the demons out is the energy shield around this city.”

Neorock’s mind was spinning. So much information… and yet, something still didn’t make sense.

He looked up at Phantom. "Then why did you come for me?"

Phantom placed a hand on Neorock’s shoulder.

"Because, Neorock… you are the last hope of the multiverse."

The Truth about Earth

Neorock’s chest tightened. "You keep saying that the Milky Way is in danger… but Earth still looks normal."

Phantom’s face turned grim.

"You don’t understand. The Milky Way is already lost."

Neorock’s blood ran cold. "What?"

Phantom turned to the sky, his eyes filled with sorrow. "The shaking you felt in your room, the strange voices you heard… that was the moment the demons arrived."

Neorock’s heart pounded. "But that means—"

Phantom nodded. "Earth, your home… is already in Arengoth’s hands. The people may not realize it yet, but the invasion has begun. If we do nothing, Earth will be completely consumed by darkness, but we can’t do anything, we don’t have that much power."

Neorock felt his entire world shatter. His home… his family… everything was already lost?

Phantom knelt beside him. "But you, Neorock, still have a chance to fight. If you train, if you grow strong… you can save them. You can save the multiverse, and Arengoth will not kill your family and other people but will make them as his worker or will keep them in prisons."

Neorock clenched his fists.

He was no hero. He was just a kid.

But if Earth was truly in danger… he couldn’t just sit back and do nothing.

Taking a deep breath, Neorock looked Phantom straight in the eye.

"I’ll do it. Train me."

Phantom gave a small smile. "Then welcome to Herlek, Neorock. Your journey begins now."

Just as Neorock was trying to process everything, a new figure approached. He was tall and wore a long dark-blue coat with silver linings. His sharp eyes studied Neorock before he smirked.

"So, this is the kid you brought, Phantom?" the man said.

Phantom nodded. "Neorock, this is Vault. He is my closest ally and one of the last survivors of Herlek."

Vault crossed his arms. "If you’re going to survive here, kid, you need proper training. And that’s where I come in."

Neorock hesitated. "You’re going to train me?"

Vault grinned. "That’s right. Your training will last ten years. If you want to fight against Arengoth, you’ll need to master your abilities first."

Neorock felt a lump in his throat. Ten years… That was longer than he had imagined. But he had already made his decision.

"I’m ready," he said.

Vault chuckled. "Good. Then let’s begin."

A Teacher Like No Other

Vault led Neorock through the futuristic city, past towering skyscrapers and glowing energy fields. As they walked, Vault spoke, his tone both serious and amused.

"You probably think training means swinging a sword and throwing punches, right?" he asked.

Neorock shrugged. "Isn’t that how warriors train?"

Vault chuckled. "That’s part of it. But before you can fight, you need to understand what you’re fighting for. Power without purpose is just destruction."

Neorock frowned. "Then what am I supposed to do?"

Vault smirked. "First, we talk. You need knowledge before you need strength."

The Weight of the Multiverse

Over the next few days, Vault and Neorock had long conversations. They spoke of the history of the Nexus Universe, the rise of Arengoth, and the countless worlds that had already fallen. Vault described battles so intense that entire planets were erased from existence.

"Arengoth doesn’t just conquer," Vault explained. "He corrupts. When he takes over a world, he doesn’t just rule it—he turns its people into his soldiers. That’s why fighting him isn’t just about strength. It’s about resisting his influence."

Neorock listened carefully, realizing that this war wasn’t just about saving Earth. It was about saving the very fabric of reality itself.

A Decade of Training

On the third day, Vault took Neorock to a massive training hall. The walls were lined with ancient weapons, energy-infused armor, and technology beyond Earth’s imagination.

"Your training begins now," Vault said, tossing Neorock a small, metallic device.

Neorock examined it. "What is this?"

Vault grinned. "A battle core. It enhances your strength, but only if you can control it. Ten years from now, you’ll be a warrior. But first, let’s see if you can survive a single day."

Neorock took a deep breath and clenched his fists. This was just the beginning.
